My Identity & Values

A situation where you have made an ethical decision & a situation where I tried to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.

I used to be a site engineer in the construction of major infrastructure here in Auckland. About nine months after starting my first job out of university, I was assigned an unexperienced crew to build a noise wall. It was difficult working with them from the beginning due to the lack of experience, not only on their part, but also mine. This often resulted in us working into the late afternoon after all the other crews had gone home.

One particular day, we were still very much in the middle of a task when an issue arose. I knew what the right thing to do was – to essentially undo some of the progress we had made that day. Something that I knew the crew would not want to hear.

The other option would have been to continue on building this portion wrong, and dealing with the issue set in structural concrete tomorrow, which would make today a good day and would avoid direct conflict with my crew.

I decided it was important to speak up and be honest. I gave the instructions to my crew, as the engineer in charge. However, they did not like it and did not want to listen to me, no matter how much I insisted on these instruction.

I realised that they would not be listening to me. I decided the best thing to do was to call for back-up. I called over to my foreman, who was not involved in this noise wall at all but did sit above anyone from the crew working on this noise wall and happened to be working only a few hundred metres away from us. As soon as he walked over, he agreed with me and gave them the same instructions I had previously given them. It was then that they complied.

I now see that there were two ethical dilemmas involved in this situation for me. The first being the decision to ensure the wall was build correctly, as per the plans. The second was standing my ground as a young female in the construction industry which was not easy.

I remember back then, I felt sad and somewhat ashamed that 1) this situation happened at all, where we started to deviate from the design and 2) that I needed someone else in order for the crew to listen to me. I think I can now see the situation for what it was – an unexperienced team trying to work together, and more seasoned workers not wanting to listen to someone half their age and female. I am proud that despite feeling inadequate at the time, I ensured my job was done well, with honesty, integrity, passion and courage.

As for the strategies when dealing with people who believe they know best, I have learnt a lot. I learnt a lot in this first job about how you can phrase things in such a way to make people feel like decisions have been made collectively, with them included in the decision-making process. I think I probably came across confrontational due to the stress and high stakes of the situation. Despite already knowing a little about how to best deal with difficult people, I was not able to put it to practice in this instance.

How my culture and the people around me have influenced my values and identity.

Being a first-generation immigrant to parent to have immigrated to not one, but two countries, assimilating two cultures completely different from their own, I think kindness is very important. Everyone has their own struggles and showing compassion for others is what being human is about.

Like I said in my story about my time as a site engineer, I have learnt to pick my battles and know when you must have the courage to put your foot down and stand up for what you believe is right.

As an immigrant woman, values like kindness, love and honesty come quite naturally to me. However, courage, which I believe to be equally important, is sometimes harder and I have consciously work on it.

Lastly, having been given the opportunities I have in life, even if my life is far from perfect, I can’t help but feel a great deal of appreciation for what I have - both in material possessions, experiences and knowledge, and who I have become because of these opportunities.

My strengths will support me in my learning journey.

Honesty, compassion, kindness and appreciation are some of my strengths. I believe this will allow me to relate to the people I come in contact with and build strong relationships.

I am also always looking for improvement and don’t believe myself to be a master of anything. I believe humility and recognising there is room to grow is key in becoming someone who always looks to better themselves and learn new things. This mindset comes in handy when studying as I am keen to learn from others and gain new skills.

My limitations, in terms of learning and career development, may affect my learning journey in several ways.

I struggle with time-management and have spent more than the recommended time on tasks as per the timebox. I am using the Pomodoro method to work on this and become more productive. I am trying to fixate on things less.

I also can get frustrated when I don’t understand something which can lead to me spending too much time on it. It also brings about feelings of self-doubt. I have already experienced this, and I have found that all I can do for now is simply keep going despite these feelings arising.
