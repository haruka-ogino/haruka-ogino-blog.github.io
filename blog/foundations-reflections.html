Foundations Core Modules Reflections

7th of Feb 2024

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

  1. I have learnt how capable I can be of change. For example, I tend to spend too long on tasks, but using the Toggle app to track this, I have found myself being much more mindful of it.
  2. I have also seen the power of the mindset. I had tried to practice something similar in the past but learning about neuroplasticity and this concept, I found myself to be much more confident in my struggle and hard work.
  3. Lastly, I was reminded of the concept of continuous improvement in all aspects of life - social, personal and professional.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Empathy allows for better collaboration between team members where collaborators can better understand each other.

Self-awareness reveals a lot of insight to oneself; such as strengths, weaknesses and knowing what one needs.

Values are what make people who they are.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

While I have always thought about the idea of continuous improvement in personal growth as something I want to practice, I realised I have not really been doing this in an effective manner. I have mostly stayed in my comfort zone but I feel like I am finally getting out of this and challenging myself.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

When I have done journalling in the past, it felt like I could keep tyoing forever. For that reason, I have been scared to start as I don't want to end up writing for hours everyday.

In saying this, I am realising the solution is liekly to set a timer and only journal for that set time - which I would like to try.

I did somewhat struggle making time for it as well. However, I was not too worried, as I did do the prep modules, reading those parts thorughly and making notes along the way which allowed me to focus a bit more on the Tech Modules during the sprints.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think this is probably the case as it is something a lot of people and education institutions neglect. However, just as we have been reminded so far in Foundations, I have also found that employers care about well-rounded people, and not the best technical skills.

At almost all my previous civil engineering jobs, they were always running courses on soft skills, such as leadership, time-management and a positive attitude.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?

It does not feel like a waste at all. I thoroughly enjoyed learning about effective ways to problem solve, the theory of neuroplasticity and of course, the growth mindset.

Taking the time to do these, instead of only programming, is important to develop other parts of the brain that also need it.
