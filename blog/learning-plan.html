My Learning Plan for Bootcamp

8th of Feb 2024

My long-term goals and career pathway

In the long term, I want to make this my primary life-long career. As a lover of maths and problem solving, I want to pursue a career that involves these. I also love Japanese, and hope to one day work a job that can combine all my passions into one.

I also really want to get involved in female in tech groups, like I have been in civil engineer. Connecting with other women in the industry, having mentors, giving insight to younger females pursuing their careers, are all very important to me.

My strengths and limitations while at Bootcamp

I previously described being thorough as one of my strengths, while also expressing concern as it could often also become bad time management. I am now feeling like I am becoming better at time management; not dwelling on specific concepts, but instead finding a balance between spending enough time to understand concepts 'enough' - about 80%. The toggle desktop app along with the timeboxes on the handbook have been great at helping me keep track of the time I spend on every task.

I had also stated that I could easily become frustrated when I struggled with something. However, after learning about the growth mindset and neuroplasticity, I am feeling much more positive when I encounter struggles. I am keeping the mindset of 'not yet' and reminding myself that you do not become good at something overnight. And that that doesn't have to reflect negative on your intelligence or effort. Having this mindset, does not mean that everything will be easy and there won't be time of struggle and frustration, I just feel better equipped to handle these.

I will keep working on these newfound strengths as I am not 100% confident in them, I just feel like I am turning these from weaknesses to strengths through conscious effort.

My biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp

Something that I am a little concerned about is the amount of time we are to dedicate to Bootcamp. I have a puppy that required a lot of attention. I also have a partner who is extremely understanding, that I would like to spend time with.

I am thinking of working extra hard on weekdays, do a few hours on Saturdays too, and minimal work on Sundays to catch up on chores and have some social life too.

I want to give Bootcamp my all and really make the most of it, so I am concerned about whether I will be able to have a balanced life. However, since it is a 9 week Bootcamp, I feel like I will need to prioritise the coursework over other aspects of my life.

The non-technical skills - human skills - I would like to see developed in myself while at Dev Academy

I want to be able to collaborate better in groups by speaking my mind. In the past, while working in groups, I have felt like my voice isn't heard. I have often felt out of place being the only female around and it is not a nice feeling. I would like to make myself heard without letting other people talk over me or disregard my ideas.

Along with this, come leadership skills. In the past, I have always held back due to feeling scared of coming across as bossy, but I want to move away from those beliefs and allow myself to develop leadership skills.

My expectations from the Bootcamp team

I want feedback regarding both my 'Core' and 'Tech' skills. Feedback to me would include areas of improvement on each, some insight into particular strengths would be helpful too.

Regular feedback and check-ins are something I am really hoping for.

Expectations of myself

  • I should be fully committed to the Bootcamp and work hard to meet my goals.
  • I want to keep on top of things via priming before the following week's content and using my time wisely.
  • I also will fully commit to the job application process like I have in the past in engineering.
  • I will ask for help after trying something for 30 min - with maybe around 5 min of helplessness before reaching out - and trying the ruber ducky debugging method before doing so!
  • I want to build relationships wih my peers so that we can help build each other up.


My Learning Plan for Foundations

18th of Jan 2024

My long-term goals and career pathway

I want to have a career in tech. I want to work in an industry that is dynamic, always looking to improve. I want to continue to learn and be active in encouraging women of colour to participate. I want to work in an industry that excites me.

My strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I think one of my strengths is that I am thorough. However, I have to be careful as this can quickly turn into bad management so I am trying to walk that fine line, trying to understand concepts, while not fixating on them.

I also know some of my weak points, such as time management, which allows me to look out for them. I am keeping my eye on my timer as I have the Toggl desktop app going every time I am working.

Accepting that some things may be hard to understand and not getting frustrated/dealing with the frustration is something I am working on.

Some skills I want to develop while at Dev Academy

I want to develop confidence in myself and my abilities. I also desperately want to become good at time-management and am actively working on it.

How I will manage my workload in this programme...

I struggle putting myself out there so I want to be a bit more outspoken than I have been in the past on discord and group discussions.

I have already started making a conscious effort to do this more in our homegroup meetings.

When things start to get difficult and busy, I will take short breaks and remind myself that this part of the learning process.

I will also work on weekend as required in order to achieve my goals.

Seeking help in a timely way

I commit to reach out for help to facilitators and my peers if I get stuck on something for longer than 20min.

My expectations from the facilitation team

I want to receive feedback on my code; I want to know if I seem to misunderstand concepts, areas of improvement (such as conciseness), etc.

My schedule

During foundations, I am the sole carer of my puppy, so I while I do not have any block-out times during work hours, I have to take some longer breaks during the day to play with her and keep her busy. I make up for these by working longer day and some Saturday mornings.
